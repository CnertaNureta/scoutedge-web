# World Cup 2026 Power Rankings — Week 7

**Published: May 6, 2026 | Edition #7 | 36 days to kick-off**

Thirty-six days. With just over five weeks until Mexico fires the opening whistle against South Africa in Mexico City, the World Cup 2026 shape is crystallising fast. This week's Power Rankings carry more information than any previous edition — squad announcements are landing daily, form from Europe's domestic run-in is cementing the final picture, and KickOracle's Chemistry Index is registering the most significant single-week movements of the pre-tournament period.

This week: Spain climb to the summit for the first time. Belgium fall out of the top five. Morocco post an all-time chemistry high. And a sleeper from Southeast Asia is now the model's biggest mover across the entire 48-team field.

---

## How to Read These Rankings

The Power Score runs from 0 to 100. A score above 85 sits in genuine title contention. Between 75 and 85 is the quarter-final-or-better tier. Between 65 and 75 is competitive — these teams will likely qualify from their group but face a ceiling in the last 16. Below 65 are the tournament's underdogs, capable of surprises but with the deck stacked against them.

Edition #7 incorporates squad announcement data, Champions League and domestic league run-in form, and the latest Chemistry Index cycle update. All scores are directional — movements reflect change since Edition #6 (April 29).

## Tier 1 — Title Contenders (Score 85+)

### 🇪🇸 #1 Spain — Power Score: ~93 ▲ (+2)

Spain lead the Power Rankings for the first time. Lamine Yamal's Champions League semi-final performance — two goals and an assist across both legs of their tie — has been the most discussed individual performance in world football this week, but KickOracle's model registers something deeper: Spain's squad chemistry has reached 89/100, the highest it has sat since we began tracking.

Luis de la Fuente announced a provisional 26-man squad on May 1 that contained zero surprises — which is itself the signal. No contested selections, no controversial inclusions, no political omissions. A manager with a settled first choice across all eleven positions and genuine depth in every line. Spain's morale score (91/100) is now the tournament's highest.

Rodri's fitness update is the single risk factor. He has been managed carefully through a minor knee complaint in April — he is expected fit, but the model carries a 12% probability of a starts-limited role in the group stage. That 12% is the only thing keeping Spain below 95 on the composite.

**KickOracle Signal**: Spain have played 23 competitive internationals under De la Fuente. Their win rate in those games is 78%. Their average Chemistry Index across those 23 games: 87/100. This is not a team peaking by accident — it is a team that has been building to exactly this moment.

### 🇦🇷 #2 Argentina — Power Score: ~92 ▼ (–0)

Argentina hold at second, fractionally behind Spain on composite. Their chemistry (90/100) remains the tournament's highest single score, and Scaloni confirmed the squad structure on May 3 — Messi included, with a managed minutes protocol that protects him through the group stage and into the knockouts.

The movement to watch: Julián Álvarez has been in the form of his life at Atlético Madrid this spring, posting 9 goals in 11 La Liga appearances since February. His club form has elevated Argentina's forward line probability distribution — KickOracle's model now gives Argentina a higher expected goals output in the group stage than any previous edition.

**KickOracle Signal**: Argentina have not lost a competitive international match in 34 games. The longest active unbeaten run of any tournament nation. The chemistry and morale data both explain it: this is not streak that happened to them — they built it deliberately.

### 🇫🇷 #3 France — Power Score: ~88 ▲ (+1)

France rise one place as Mbappé's Real Madrid form enters the model at its highest-ever rating. His 2025-26 Champions League output — 11 goals and 7 assists entering the final — has pushed his individual performance score to 9.1/10, the highest of any outfield player tracked in the World Cup cohort.

Deschamps confirmed a 28-man preliminary squad that will narrow to 26 by May 20. The one unresolved selection: the backup goalkeeper spot. Both Pau Lopez and Brice Samba are included. The model treats this as negligible — France's defensive structure does not depend on goalkeeper quality in the way some sides do.

**KickOracle Signal**: France are the only team with three players rated above 8.5/10 on individual contribution (Mbappé, Tchouaméni, Camavinga). Depth of this quality translates to tournament durability — they are the model's most likely team to peak in the semi-finals rather than the group stage.

---

### 🇩🇪 #4 Germany — Power Score: ~87 ▲ (+2)

Germany are the week's biggest riser among the Tier 1 group. Nagelsmann's Bundesliga-season-closing form — four wins from five for his key club players — combined with a chemistry update following the squad camp in late April has pushed Die Mannschaft up two points. Their Chemistry Index now sits at 82/100, up from 79 in the inaugural edition.

The Florian Wirtz situation is resolved: he is fit and in the squad, having overcome a minor muscular issue that had been flagged amber in our system since mid-April. With Wirtz and Musiala both confirmed available, Germany's creative output model recovers its full ceiling.

**KickOracle Signal**: Germany's 2024 Euro host-nation performance generated something the data had not captured before — a home tournament environment produced measurable improvements in squad morale and execution under pressure. That learning is now embedded. The model weights Germany's knockout-round probability higher than their group stage form alone would suggest.

## Tier 2 — Dark Horses (Score 75–84)

### 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 #5 England — Power Score: ~83 ▲ (+1)

England rise to fifth as Tuchel's system takes root. Six months of international camps have produced a measurable chemistry improvement — England's Index moved from 77/100 to 80/100 this week, the largest single-edition jump in the top ten. Jude Bellingham's role as the system's creative fulcrum is now understood and drilled to a degree that was not the case when Tuchel started.

The formation settled in the April friendly window: a 4-3-3 with Bellingham in the right halfspace, Saka and Foden providing width, and Kane as the focal point. KickOracle's model rates this system as England's highest-ever tactical coherence reading.

**KickOracle Signal**: England's gap between talent ceiling (estimated ~91) and current chemistry (80) is closing faster than the model expected. Tuchel has six weeks. If the Chemistry Index reaches 84+ by June 11, England become a genuine tournament dark horse rather than a credible quarter-final exit.

### 🇵🇹 #6 Portugal — Power Score: ~81 ▼ (–0)

Portugal hold steady at sixth. Roberto Martinez's provisional 27-man list, announced May 2, confirms the post-Ronaldo squad direction: no single creative fulcrum, instead a distributed system built around Leão's wide threat, Bernardo Silva's positional intelligence, and Bruno Fernandes' set-piece leverage.

The one concern the model flags: Portugal's Schedule Difficulty in Group B is the tournament's third-highest. They open against two teams (Belgium, Morocco) who both rank inside the model's top 20. Win both and they are possibly the tournament's form side — exit early from Group B and the whole campaign collapses.

### 🇧🇷 #7 Brazil — Power Score: ~80 ▲ (+1)

Brazil's tactical clarity has improved. Dorival Júnior's May 1 squad announcement — notably, his selection of Endrick as the starting centre-forward — resolves the ambiguity that had kept Brazil's stability score at 58/100 for three consecutive editions. The model updates Brazil's stability to 64/100 following the clear striker selection.

Vinicius Jr is confirmed, fit, and having his best ever month of European club football. Their Chemistry Index of 74/100 remains below the title-contenders group, but the directional momentum — up 1 point per edition for the last three editions — is the signal to watch.

**KickOracle Signal**: Brazil's forward trio (Vinicius, Raphinha, Endrick) is the highest-rated attacking unit in the model when all three are at form peak. The 24-year title drought creates enormous pressure, but pressure and morale are not the same thing — Brazil's morale score of 80/100 reflects a squad that believes, not a squad that is suffocating under weight.

### 🇧🇪 #8 Belgium — Power Score: ~78 ▼ (–5) ⚠ FALLER OF THE WEEK

Belgium drop five points — the biggest negative movement of any team this edition. The cause: Kevin De Bruyne withdrew from the Manchester City end-of-season squad on May 3 with a "precautionary" hamstring management programme. Roberto Martinez confirmed De Bruyne is in the World Cup squad but on a fitness protocol that limits full training until May 20.

KickOracle's model is conservative about precautionary management at this stage of the calendar. De Bruyne's absence from 12 days of full training, at 35 years old, with a hamstring concern, is a yellow flag that cannot be waved away. Belgium's Chemistry Index falls to 74/100 (from 79) in the model's availability-adjusted calculation.

**KickOracle Signal**: Belgium without De Bruyne at full fitness is a genuinely different team. Their model score drops from ~83 to ~72 in scenarios where he starts but is limited to 70-minute performances. This is the most significant single-player availability risk in the tournament.

### 🇳🇱 #9 Netherlands — Power Score: ~79 ▼ (–1)

Netherlands drop slightly — not through any bad news, but because the sides around them have strengthened. Koeman's team remain a pragmatic, defensively solid quarter-final tier side. Van Dijk leads their defensive Chemistry (87/100 — the tournament's highest for a defensive unit). Memphis Depay's fitness remains amber in the system.

### 🇮🇹 #10 Italy — Power Score: ~78 ▲ (+1)

The Azzurri hold their position with a modest improvement after Mancini's squad confirmation. Their Chemistry Index of 82/100 (high for their FIFA ranking position) reflects a squad with exceptional collective cohesion, built through two years of consistent selection and system drilling. Italy remain one of the model's most underestimated knockout-round sides.

## Tier 3 — Competitive (Score 65–74)

### Notable Movers

**🇲🇦 Morocco (#11, ~77) ▲ (+3) — CLIMBER OF THE WEEK**

Morocco post an all-time Chemistry Index high of 88/100 this edition — the joint-highest chemistry score ever recorded for a non-top-six side. Regragui's April training camp, which included three full-squad sessions focused on defensive set-pieces and transition press, produced a measurable cohesion improvement. The model now treats Morocco's defensive unit as the tournament's second-best organised defensive system (behind Italy).

Their upset probability against Portugal in Group B has risen from 31% to 38% in the current model cycle.

**🇺🇸 USA (#12, ~76) ▲ (+2)**

The co-hosts are being consistently underestimated. Home advantage is real and measurable — KickOracle's home-field model allocates a +4.5 points composite boost to the USA for their group-stage games (all played in North America). Pulisic's form at AC Milan has pushed his individual score to 8.3/10. Their Chemistry Index (72/100) remains modest, but home crowd energy is a separate variable that compounds significantly.

**🇯🇵 Japan (#13, ~74) ▲ (+4) — BIGGEST OVERALL MOVER**

Japan are the edition's biggest overall mover — up four points to 74/100. Their May 3 friendly against Paraguay produced a 3-0 win that the model registers as the highest-quality performance by any team outside the top 10 in the pre-tournament window. Pressing intensity, defensive compactness, and directness: the numbers suggest a Japanese side at the peak of their collective development. At 74/100, they now sit firmly in "dangerous last-16 opponent" territory.

**🇨🇴 Colombia (#14, ~74) ▲ (+1)**

James Rodríguez's club form at São Paulo has kept the model's individual contribution score elevated. Copa America 2024 champions enter as South America's third representative and routinely underrated. Group stage draw is favourable.

**🇲🇽 Mexico (#15, ~72) ▼ (–1)**

Co-hosts, but the data has not moved Mexico upward this edition. Their Chemistry Index (68/100) is the lowest of any home nation, and Javier Aguirre's tactical system remains defensively cautious in a way that limits their ceiling. The Azteca crowd advantage is unquantifiable — and that's the only thing between Mexico and a group-stage exit scare.

## The Chemistry Clock

36 days to kickoff is the point where Chemistry Index movements become the most predictive input in the model. Squad announcements are finalised or finalising. Tactical systems are locked. The last major external input — final friendlies (May 26–June 8) — is the only remaining event that could shift scores meaningfully.

What to watch over the next three weeks:
- **De Bruyne fitness**: If he returns to full training by May 20, Belgium recover most of their lost ground.
- **Rodri**: Spain's score ceiling depends on a full fitness confirmation.
- **Brazil's chemistry trajectory**: Three consecutive editions of upward movement. If that continues to June, Brazil enter the tournament as dark horses, not underdogs.
- **Japan's pressing system**: The May 3 Paraguay performance may not be a one-off. Worth watching their final May friendlies closely.
