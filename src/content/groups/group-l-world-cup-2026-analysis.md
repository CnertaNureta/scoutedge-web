# World Cup 2026 Group L Analysis: England vs Croatia — Two Title Contenders, One Group

Group L of the 2026 FIFA World Cup packages two of the game's most compelling narratives into the same group stage bracket. England — 60 years after their only World Cup triumph, under mounting pressure with a squad that finally has the depth to match its ambition — face Croatia, veterans of two recent World Cup semi-finals, one final, and a squad built around the extraordinary Luka Modrić. Ghana bring African physicality and their Black Stars' flair. Panama carry CONCACAF spirit and the belief that nothing is impossible when you're playing your rivals at the World Cup.

## Group L Overview

Group L is one of the World Cup's most high-profile groups — England's global fanbase and Croatia's tournament pedigree guarantee enormous attention in every match. England should advance as group winners; Croatia's veteran core around Modrić makes them the most likely second qualifier. Ghana and Panama will compete for the third-place playoff slot and the chance to be the group's giant-killers.

## Team-by-Team Breakdown

### England 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 — 60 Years of Hurt and the Squad to End It

**FIFA Ranking**: #4 | **Coach**: Thomas Tuchel | **World Cup Titles**: 1 (1966)

"Football's coming home" has been England's anthem of ironic hope for three decades. But entering 2026, something feels different. England reached the Euro 2020 final, the 2022 World Cup quarter-finals, and Euro 2024 final — an unprecedented run of tournament consistency for a generation that was supposed to be perpetually falling short. The squad now features world-class quality in every line, and the psychological barrier of semi-finals has been broken.

Jude Bellingham's emergence as one of the world's best players gives England a creative dynamism from midfield that Beckham's generation couldn't provide. Harry Kane, whether at his Bayern Munich best or managing fitness at 32, remains one of the world's most reliable goalscorers — his movement and technical finishing are unmatched in the England setup. Phil Foden's City-formed creativity, Bukayo Saka's directness and relentlessness, and a defensive unit anchored by John Stones' intelligent positional play give England a complete squad package.

**Strengths**: Bellingham's world-class creative quality, Kane's clinical finishing, Saka and Foden's attacking variety, defensive Premier League depth, tournament experience through recent semi-finals and finals.

**Weaknesses**: England have historically underperformed relative to expectation at major tournaments. The psychological weight of 60 years without a World Cup title is real. Their penalty record has been catastrophic at crucial moments.

**ScoutEdge assessment**: England have the squad to go all the way — but so have previous England squads. What's different in 2026 is the combination of tournament experience (they've been to finals and semi-finals without imploding), individual quality at the highest club level, and a coach who has brought genuine tactical clarity. Group L should be won. The knockout rounds are where it gets interesting.

### Croatia 🇭🇷 — Modrić's Final Act

**FIFA Ranking**: #13 | **Coach**: Zlatko Dalić | **Best finish**: Finalists 2018, 3rd place 2022

Croatia are one of the great modern World Cup stories. A nation of four million people, they reached the 2018 World Cup final, losing to France. They finished third in 2022, beating Morocco in a match that closed one chapter and opened another. The architect of both runs — Luka Modrić, now 40 years old at the tournament — may finally be playing his last major international competition.

Zlatko Dalić has built his Croatia around Modrić's creative intelligence, and that approach hasn't wavered. Croatia are extraordinarily disciplined tactically, physically aggressive across the squad, and psychologically extremely strong — particularly in knockout football, where their experience at tournaments is matched by few nations in Europe.

The transition beyond Modrić has been the question hanging over Croatian football for years. Mateo Kovačić provides the technical and physical bridge. Andrej Kramarić continues scoring at club level. But the key question is: can Croatia win a World Cup group-stage match on collective momentum when Modrić isn't at his transcendent best?

**Strengths**: Modrić's creative genius and leadership, tournament experience (two semi-finals in consecutive World Cups), tactical discipline, Dalić's outstanding coaching quality.

**Weaknesses**: Age at the back and in midfield. The transition generation isn't yet at the level of their predecessors. Without Modrić playing 90 minutes at his best, Croatia's creative output is significantly reduced.

**ScoutEdge assessment**: Croatia will likely finish second in Group L. Their experience and organizational quality should see them through, but England's superior squad depth means second place is more realistic than first. In the knockouts, Croatia remain extraordinarily dangerous — their ability to manage games, win penalties, and perform in shootouts is historically proven.

### Ghana 🇬🇭 — Black Stars' Ambition

**FIFA Ranking**: #47 | **Coach**: Otto Addo | **Best finish**: Quarter-finals (2010)

Ghana's 2010 quarter-final run remains one of African football's greatest moments — Asamoah Gyan's agonizing penalty miss against Uruguay, Luis Suárez's handball, and the continent's heartbreak at being denied a first-ever World Cup semi-final. The current Ghana squad carries that legacy and the motivation to go one step further.

The mix of European-based Ghanaian players — particularly those from Germany, England, and the Netherlands — gives the Black Stars technical quality and physical intensity. Thomas Partey's midfield dominance and experience, Mohammed Kudus's explosive quality from wide attacking positions, and Jordan Ayew's experienced leadership provide the foundations of a competitive squad.

**Strengths**: Kudus's individual brilliance, Partey's midfield authority, European club-quality throughout the squad, historical big-game experience, physical intensity.

**Weaknesses**: Ghana's consistency can be inconsistent — their 2022 campaign started with a famous win over South Korea but ended with a disjointed group-stage exit. Defensive organization can be exposed by quick wide play.

**ScoutEdge assessment**: Ghana have the quality to advance from this group — but England and Croatia are both better on paper. Their target is likely a third-place position that could qualify them through the best third-place route. The Panama match is their most realistic chance of a clear win; competitive performances against England and Croatia could earn the point they need.

### Panama 🇵🇦 — CONCACAF's Passionate Warriors

**FIFA Ranking**: #49 | **Coach**: Thomas Christiansen | **Best finish**: Group stage (2018 debut)

Panama made their World Cup debut in 2018 in Russia, losing all three matches but scoring a famous goal through Felipe Baloy against England — a player who came out of international retirement specifically for the World Cup, scored, and celebrated with one of the tournament's most joyful dances. In 2026, they return as a more experienced side under Danish coach Thomas Christiansen.

The quality gap between Panama and England or Croatia is significant, but Panama's physical intensity and organizational discipline under Christiansen makes them harder to break down than their ranking suggests. They will compete with everything they have and could make England's group-stage opener uncomfortable.

**Strengths**: Physical intensity, organized defensive discipline, team spirit, set-piece threat, fearless mentality against bigger nations.

**Weaknesses**: Attacking quality is limited. Panama struggle to create meaningful chances against organized defenses, and their route to points is narrow — set pieces and counter-attacks are their primary weapons.

**ScoutEdge assessment**: Panama will compete with spirit and organization. The Ghana match is their most realistic chance of points. Against England and Croatia, they'll aim to frustrate and make the match competitive, but the quality gap is difficult to bridge over 90 minutes.

## Key Matches to Watch in Group L

### England vs Croatia

The group's defining match and one of the World Cup's most anticipated group-stage encounters. England and Croatia have recent history: they met in the 2018 World Cup semi-final (Croatia won), the Euro 2020 final group stage (England won), and Euro 2024 group stage (England won). The narrative writes itself. Bellingham vs Modrić — new generation vs legend. England's pressing versus Croatia's intelligent game management. A match where one result changes the bracket for both sides' knockout journey.

### England vs Ghana

England should win — but Ghana's Kudus at his best is a match-winner. England's fans will expect a comfortable result; Ghana's ambition and physical intensity could make it tight. The first half will determine everything.

### Croatia vs Ghana

The battle for second place. Modrić's creativity versus Partey's midfield authority. Croatia's tournament experience versus Ghana's physical intensity. This match could come down to a single moment of quality from either side's best player.

## Our Dark Horse Pick: Croatia 🇭🇷

Never underestimate a Zlatko Dalić Croatia team in a World Cup knockout round. Their psychological strength in shootouts, their tactical discipline over 90 and 120 minutes, and the individual genius of Modrić in his final tournament create a team that is more than the sum of its parts. If Croatia reach the Round of 16, they are a quarterfinal threat regardless of opponent. Modrić's farewell could be one of the tournament's great narratives if Croatia find one last deep run.

## Frequently Asked Questions: World Cup 2026 Group L

**Who will qualify from World Cup 2026 Group L?**
England are the favorites to top Group L. Croatia, with Modrić's experience and Dalić's tactical intelligence, are expected to advance as second. Ghana and Panama will compete for the third-place playoff slot.

**What is England's World Cup record?**
England are World Cup champions, having won the trophy in 1966 on home soil. Since then, they've reached the quarter-finals multiple times and the semi-final in 2018. The 2026 tournament represents their strongest squad in decades.

**What is Luka Modrić's age at World Cup 2026?**
Luka Modrić will be 40 years old during the 2026 World Cup. He may well participate — he continued playing at Real Madrid at an extraordinary level well into his late 30s. If selected, the 2026 World Cup will almost certainly be his last.

**What is Croatia's World Cup record?**
Croatia reached the World Cup final in 2018, losing 4-2 to France. In 2022, they finished third, beating Morocco in the third-place match. With six World Cup appearances since their 1998 debut, Croatia have been remarkably consistent for such a small nation.

**What is Ghana's best World Cup finish?**
Ghana reached the quarter-finals of the 2010 World Cup in South Africa, becoming only the third African team to reach the quarter-finals after Cameroon (1990) and Senegal (2002). Their elimination — when Uruguay goalkeeper Suárez handled on the line and Asamoah Gyan missed the resulting penalty — remains one of the most controversial moments in World Cup history.

**Has Panama beaten England at a World Cup?**
Yes — at the 2018 World Cup in Russia, Panama scored a famous goal against England through Felipe Baloy in a match England won 6-1. Despite the scoreline, Baloy's goal was celebrated wildly across Panama as a historic first World Cup goal for the nation.
