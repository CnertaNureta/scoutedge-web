# World Cup 2026 Group B Preview: Portugal's to Lose, Iran and Cameroon Battle for Second

Group B of the 2026 World Cup presents a clear hierarchy but a genuinely compelling subplot. Portugal headline as one of the tournament's pre-eminent contenders, but the three-way battle between Iran, Cameroon, and Bolivia for the remaining knockout spots offers intrigue.

## The Teams

### Portugal (FIFA #6, Elo #4)
The overwhelming favorites. Bernardo Silva, Bruno Fernandes, Rafael Leão, and João Neves form a midfield and attacking unit that would grace any squad at the tournament. Cristiano Ronaldo at 41 adds star power and an unmatchable will to win. Portugal's 55% possession average and 79% pass completion rate reflect a team that controls matches. The 3.7 xG across three matches is solid, and with Leão and Neto stretching defenses, the attacking output should increase against Group B opponents.

### Iran (FIFA #20, Elo #20)
The most organized challenger. Iran's 59% possession and 82% pass completion suggest a team that has evolved beyond counter-attacking pragmatism. Mehdi Taremi (Inter Milan) and Sardar Azmoun (Roma) form a striker partnership with genuine European pedigree. The 4.0 xG across three matches indicates clinical finishing ability.

### Cameroon (FIFA #48, Elo #31)
The most dangerous wildcard. Cameroon's 4.6 xG is the highest of any Group B team, and with André Onana (Manchester United) in goal and Zambo Anguissa (Napoli) driving the midfield, they have match-winners at both ends. Bryan Mbeumo's emergence as a Premier League goal-threat adds a new dimension.

### Bolivia (FIFA #82, Elo 1,438)
The romantic returnees. After 32 years away from the World Cup, Bolivia's domestic-league squad faces a significant quality gap at sea level. Marcelo Moreno Martins' experience and Carlos Lampe's goalkeeping offer hope of competitive performances.

## Key Matchup: Iran vs Cameroon

This is the group's decisive fixture. Iran's organization against Cameroon's explosive attacking talent. Iran will look to control possession and limit space, while Cameroon will press high and use pace in transition. The winner likely secures second place; the loser faces an anxious wait for best third-place calculations.
