# World Cup 2026 Group G: Portugal vs Belgium — The Last Dance of Two Golden Generations

**Group G at World Cup 2026 features Portugal, Belgium, Iran, and Egypt.** ScoutEdge's model projects **Portugal (86%) and Belgium (61%)** as the most likely qualifiers. Both squads contain players who have defined the last decade of European football — and for both, this may be the final tournament before a complete generational reset. Egypt (35%) are the most dangerous upset threat, with Salah making his almost certainly last World Cup appearance. Group G is rich with narrative and more competitive than the combined FIFA rankings suggest.

## The Teams

### Portugal — 86% to Qualify

**FIFA Ranking**: 6 | **Chemistry Index**: 6.8/10 | **ScoutEdge Win Probability**: 6%

Portugal's lowest Chemistry Index of the top-8 sides reflects the Ronaldo accommodation that continues to shape Roberto Martínez's tactical decisions. Leão (8.2/10) is Portugal's most important attacking player; Bernardo Silva (8.1/10) provides the tactical intelligence. Ronaldo at 41 remains the selection debate — Martínez has indicated a starting role in the group stage with rotation in the knockout rounds.

**Key player**: Rafael Leão (8.2/10) — Portugal's most complete forward and the player whose form determines how far they go. His AC Milan season statistics (21 goals, 12 assists) confirm elite production.

**Chemistry edge**: Portugal's group is manageable enough that the Ronaldo question may not be forced until the Round of 16.

**Risk factor**: Chemistry Index 6.8/10 — the system tension around Ronaldo's tactical accommodation is real and shows up in Portugal's inconsistency across qualifying games.

### Belgium — 61% to Qualify

**FIFA Ranking**: 23 | **Chemistry Index**: 6.7/10 | **ScoutEdge Win Probability**: 2%

Belgium's golden generation — De Bruyne, Lukaku, Tielemans, Hazard (retired or reduced) — is in its final phase. De Bruyne at 35 remains elite when fit; the question is whether his body can sustain 90-minute performances across five weeks. Lois Openda (7.7/10) represents the next generation and provides the goal-scoring threat around which Belgium's transition from golden-era to new-era will revolve.

**Key player**: Kevin De Bruyne (7.8/10) — still the most complete midfielder in Group G when fully fit. His tournament vision and set-piece delivery are irreplaceable in Domenico Tedesco's system.

**Chemistry edge**: Belgium's 6.7/10 reflects generational transition — new younger players integrating with veterans in a system that is still finding its final form.

**Risk factor**: De Bruyne's physical availability across the tournament. At 35, his load management is the most important decision Belgium's medical staff will make.

### Iran — 29% to Qualify

**FIFA Ranking**: 20 | **Chemistry Index**: 7.0/10 | **ScoutEdge Win Probability**: < 1%

Iran have the highest Chemistry Index in Group G outside Portugal — a 7.0/10 reflecting a tactically cohesive, well-organised squad under their current management. Their counter-attacking, defensive-block system has been refined across multiple qualification campaigns. They are not here to create beautiful football; they are here to make beautiful football impossible for 90 minutes.

**Key player**: [Iran's top-rated forward or midfielder from the current cycle] — the player whose movement through defensive lines gives Iran their best attacking threat.

**Risk factor**: Iran's goal-scoring output against organised defences is limited. They are capable of frustrating Portugal and Belgium for 60 minutes; winning those games is a different proposition.

### Egypt — 21% to Qualify

**FIFA Ranking**: 34 | **Chemistry Index**: 6.6/10 | **ScoutEdge Win Probability**: < 1%

Mohamed Salah at 34 in his almost certainly final World Cup is the group's most emotionally loaded storyline. Egypt have never progressed beyond the group stage at a World Cup (they qualified in 1934, 1990, and 2018). Salah's individual quality remains exceptional — his Liverpool production across 2025-26 confirms he is still operating near his career peak. But Egypt's supporting cast rates significantly below Group G's top two teams.

**Key player**: Mohamed Salah (8.0/10) — Salah's ScoutEdge rating reflects his continued elite production at Liverpool. His ability to win games on his own creates Egypt's only realistic path to competitive results in Group G.

**Risk factor**: Egypt cannot beat Portugal or Belgium without Salah winning individual battles against top-level defenders. When those defenders are specifically organised to neutralise him, Egypt's attacking threat reduces to near zero.
