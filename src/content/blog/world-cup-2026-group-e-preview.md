# World Cup 2026 Group E: Germany's to Win — Ivory Coast the Biggest Threat

**Group E at World Cup 2026 features Germany, Ivory Coast, Ecuador, and Curaçao.** ScoutEdge's model projects **Germany (92%) and Ivory Coast (58%)** as the most likely qualifiers. ## The Teams

### Germany — 92% to Qualify

**FIFA Ranking**: 12 | **Chemistry Index**: 7.3/10 | **ScoutEdge Win Probability**: 8%

The Wirtz-Musiala axis makes Germany one of the most attractive teams in the tournament to watch. Julian Nagelsmann's system — a high-energy 4-2-3-1 that presses aggressively and transitions at pace — gives Germany a dynamic attacking identity that their previous generations lacked. The question is not whether Germany qualify from Group E; it's how efficiently they manage their top players for the knockout rounds.

**Key player**: Florian Wirtz (8.5/10) — the tournament's third-highest rated player (behind Mbappé and Haaland) and Germany's most important creative asset. His ability to combine with Musiala in tight spaces creates passing triangles that world-class defences struggle to contain.

**Chemistry edge**: Germany's 7.3/10 Chemistry Index reflects two years of consistent Nagelsmann system work. Their tactical identity is clear, automated, and well-understood across the squad.

**Risk factor**: Defensive structure under pressure. Germany's high-pressing system leaves space behind the fullbacks that transition-capable teams exploit.

## The Narrative

Group E is Germany's redemption group — but redemption from what? Their 7th-place finish in Qatar was the nadir. The pressure of hosting Euro 2024 and reaching the quarterfinals before losing to Spain has rebuilt German football's emotional confidence. Group E is the platform for Wirtz and Musiala to show the world what the new Germany looks like.

Ivory Coast's presence is the tournament's most interesting mid-tier story. A former AFCON champion with European-based talent and a counter-attacking system built to surprise — if they qualify from Group E, they enter the knockout rounds as a genuine upset threat.
