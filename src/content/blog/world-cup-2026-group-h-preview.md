## The Teams

### Spain — 91% to Qualify

Spain's Chemistry Index of 8.5/10 is the highest of any team in the tournament. Their tactical stability (8.7/10), squad familiarity (8.4/10), and morale (8.3/10) sub-scores all reflect a generation of players who grew up in the same youth system, understood each other's movement automatically, and won together at youth level before dominating at senior level. Lamine Yamal at 18 is the tournament's joint-highest rated player at 8.9/10. Pedri, Rodri (if fit), and Dani Olmo complete the most technically sophisticated midfield in Group H by some distance.

**Key player**: Lamine Yamal (8.9/10) — the Barcelona winger won Euro 2024 as the youngest European Championship winner in history and arrives at the 2026 World Cup as the tournament's most anticipated individual performer. His dribbling quality, crossing precision, and composure in big moments are, at 18, genuinely exceptional.

**Chemistry edge**: 8.5/10 — the highest in the tournament. Spain's tactical automation means every player knows exactly what every teammate will do in every pressing scenario and every transition. This collective intelligence is more valuable than individual stars, and it shows.

**Risk factor**: Rodri's fitness. Without him, Spain's defensive transition coverage drops significantly and the system becomes more vulnerable to counter-attacks.

### Serbia — 56% to Qualify

Dragan Džajić's system is built on defensive solidarity and transition pace, recognising that Serbia cannot out-possess Spain.

**Key player**: Aleksandar Mitrović (7.5/10) — the Saudi Arabian league striker's aerial ability and positioning make him Serbia's primary goal threat and the player most likely to trouble Spain's advanced defensive line.

**Chemistry edge**: Serbia's 6.9/10 reflects a squad that has maintained consistent defensive organisation across the qualification cycle. Their set-piece routine quality is explicitly captured in their tactical stability score.

**Risk factor**: Serbia cannot match Spain's technical quality across 90 minutes. If the game becomes open, Spain's passing triangles will create an insurmountable quality gap.
