## The Narrative

Group C is the World Cup 2026 group that most cleanly tells the story of modern international football: the South American giant, the African underdog who refuses to be treated as one, the plucky European qualifier, and the historic debutant.

**Morocco's 2022 legacy**: Their semifinal run redefined expectations for African football. The question heading into 2026 is whether they maintained the collective cohesion that made it possible. The data says yes — their Chemistry Index of 7.2/10 across back-to-back AFCON campaigns reflects a squad that has stayed largely together. The players who neutralised Portugal's attack in Qatar are still here.

**Brazil's 24-year drought**: The pressure on Brazil is genuinely extraordinary. They have not won a World Cup since 2002 despite having, by most metrics, the best individual talent in the world in multiple tournaments. The narrative weight of that drought will follow them into every game.

**Scotland's history**: Scotland have qualified for their third consecutive major tournament — a feat they last achieved in the 1990s. Their presence in Group C is a genuine achievement, and the match against Brazil at the MetLife Stadium will be watched by a Scottish diaspora across the USA.

**The dark horse case for Morocco**: If Morocco can hold Brazil to a draw on June 17, the group becomes genuinely open. A Morocco win would be historically seismic. At 7.2/10 Chemistry Index and with a defensive structure that has handled bigger attacks, it is not impossible. Our model gives it 14% — enough to make watching it worth your time.
